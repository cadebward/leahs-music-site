My Goal as Your Teacher

My goal as a voice teacher is to help my students learn about their natural voice, gain better breath support, increase their vocal range, become more comfortable with performances and auditions, and become more musically aware. My students are able to try several different genres and I encourage creativity.

About Leah

Soprano Leah Ward has been singing since before she could talk. From making up songs around the house to singing “Oh What a Beautiful Morning” out her bedroom window as a 4 year old, the love of song has always been with her. As a young child she was involved in the International Children’s Choir based out of Salt Lake City. With them, she sang multiple times a week for local and international dignitaries. Leah was very involved in music through her Junior High and High School years. She played the cello in her school orchestras as well as being heavily involved in the Woods Cross High School Madrigal choir and Concert choir. She went on to get a degree in Vocal Performance at Utah State University. Leah was part of both the USU Chamber Singers as well as the USU Opera program. Being part of these groups gave her several opportunities for solo performances. After graduating Leah had the opportunity to sing under the direction of Dr. Craig Jessop in the American Festival Chorus based in Logan, Utah. She has sung around the world on stages in the United States, Canada, Mexico, and China.

Specializations

Leah specializes in several musical genres such as classical, opera, musical theatre, and choral music. She is also proficient in music theory. She has 10 years of private vocal training and has been teaching private voice lessons for 3 years.
